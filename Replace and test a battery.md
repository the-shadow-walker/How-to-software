# Replace and test a battery

Replacing and testing a battery is very simple, but you need to know a few core things.

> [!warning] Never hold a battery by the leads/wires
> If you do hold them by the wires, they can disconnect at bad times or be at the wrong distance and arc, and then magical smoke goes everywhere.

> [!warning] Don't drop it
> If the battery is dropped from over 2 ft we have to throw it away. If you do drop it, please tell a mentor, or a lead who will tell a mentor, so that magical smoke doesn't escape the battery during a match.

## How to unplug a battery
1. **Hit the breaker** — Press the red button/switch on the robot labeled "BREAKER".
2. **Move the strap aside** — First remove the strap that goes across the battery, just get it out of the way, don't remove the strap all the way.
3. **Pull it out by the battery** — Next pull the battery out BY THE BATTERY!!! (not by the red or black wires, if it's stuck and the only holding spot is the wires, go grab a lead or someone who has done technician at a competition).
4. **Carry it to the cart** — When you have it out, carry it with both hands and go to the battery cart and plug it in to charge.

## How to test and plug a battery in
1. **Unplug a charged battery** — Find a battery on a charger and unplug it, again stressing to not put too much tension on the leads.
2. **Check it with the Battery Beak** — Next grab the "Battery Beak" and plug it into the battery you just unplugged. Click the yellow button, wait for the menu to appear, and then click it again. It will tell you the percentage the battery is at. A fully charged battery will be 130%, and the lowest you really want to use is 110%, this is not a hard limit, it just means the battery won't have to be replaced for another reasonable amount of time.
3. **Carry it to the robot** — When you find a battery that is at a significant amount of charge, lift it out of the battery charging bay by the battery part, and then carry it over to the robot with 2 hands.
4. **Mind the strap** — When placing the battery in, be conscious about how the battery strap will go around the battery (just don't place the battery on top of the strap in a way where it would be hard to get the strap back over).
5. **Strap it down** — When the battery is in the robot, put the strap on around the battery. Make sure the battery leads are not under the strap themselves, so weave the strap to go between the 2 leads.
6. **Plug it into the robot** — Plug the battery into the robot, this part will vary quite a bit between robots, but there will always be the robot plugin location right next to the battery compartment.
7. **Flip the breaker back on** — Finally, flip the switch on the robot breaker back in, this part can be hard to describe so ask the standing software lead how to turn the robot on.
