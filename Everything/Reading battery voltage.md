# Reading battery voltage

Reading a battery's charge is quick once you know the tool and the numbers. This is basically the same thing covered in [[Replace and test a battery]], just zoomed in on the "is this battery good?" part.

## The tool: the Battery Beak

We don't read voltage by eyeballing it, we use a little device called the **"Battery Beak."** It tells you the state of charge as a percentage, plus some other health info if you want it.

## How to read a battery
1. **Unplug a battery** — Find a battery on a charger and unplug it. Remember, don't put too much tension on the leads, pull from the connector, not the wires.
2. **Plug in the Battery Beak** — Grab the Battery Beak and plug it into the battery you just unplugged.
3. **Read it** — Click the yellow button, wait for the menu to appear, then click it again.
4. **Check the percentage** — It will show you the percentage the battery is at.

## What the numbers mean
- A **fully charged battery reads 130%.** (Yes it's over 100%, that's just how the Beak scales it, don't overthink it.)
- The **lowest you really want to use is 110%.** This isn't a hard limit, it just means the battery has a reasonable amount of life left before it'll need to come out and go back on the charger.
- Anything noticeably below that, put it back on the charger and grab a different one. Using a low battery means you'll be swapping it again really soon, and a low battery under load can sag enough to brown out the robot.

## After you've picked a good one

Once you find a battery at a significant amount of charge, lift it out of the charging bay **by the battery part** (not the leads), carry it over to the robot with **both hands**, and put it in. The full install steps, strap, plug, and breaker, are in [[Replace and test a battery]].

For the safety side of all this, see [[Safe battery handling]].
