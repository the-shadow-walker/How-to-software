# Safe battery handling

Handling a battery safely is mostly common sense, but a few of these rules are non-negotiable because getting them wrong is how the magical smoke escapes. This is basically the same stuff covered in [[Replace and test a battery]], just focused on the safety side.

## The two rules you can never break

> [!warning] Never hold a battery by the leads/wires
> If you hold the battery by the wires, two bad things can happen. One, they can disconnect at the worst possible time (like mid-match). Two, if the bare ends get to the wrong distance from each other they can arc, and then the magical smoke goes everywhere. Always carry the battery by the actual battery, with both hands.

> [!warning] Don't drop it
> If the battery is dropped from over 2 ft we have to throw it away, no exceptions. A drop can damage the cells on the inside where you can't see it, and a damaged battery can let its magical smoke escape during a match. If you do drop it, please tell a mentor, or tell a lead who will tell a mentor. Don't just put it back on the cart and hope nobody notices, because then it ends up in a robot later.

## Carrying and storing
- Always carry with **both hands**, holding the battery body, never the leads.
- When you set it down or plug it into the charging bay, lift it in and out by the battery part, not the wires.

## Before you ever pull a battery off the robot
- Press the red button/switch on the robot labeled "BREAKER" first. This kills the power so you're not unplugging a battery that still is running (not huge deal and pretty short time that this would happen, but if there is still a current running through, as you pull the battery wire out of the outlet, the space can lead to an arc, this is a pretty minor issue and is not the end of the world if you forget this step, just a good habit).
- Only then start removing the strap and the battery itself (the full step-by-step for that is in [[Replace and test a battery]]).

## If something looks wrong
If the top of the battery is bulging, abnormally hot (batteries can sometimes be warm after use and that's normal), smells weird, smoking or has damaged leads (most likely to happen) **do not use it.** Leave it where it is and grab a mentor. When in doubt, ask.
