# Connect controllers

Once again, this is a simple task once you get it.

## Connecting Stadia controllers

As of 2026, Stadia controllers are the main controllers used on our team. They look like this:

![[Pasted image 20260610145205.png|345]]

We generally connect through cables, though there is a kind of messy way we can use Bluetooth, which I won't get into.

1. **Open the Driver Station** — First open the FRC Driver Station.
2. **Plug in and untangle** — Make sure that the controller is plugged in and that the cables aren't in a tangle. That way, if you have to move the controller quickly, it doesn't unplug because it didn't have enough slack, and you can also tell what is plugged into which. I have definitely thought a controller or cable was broken because it wasn't turning on and connecting, but really I had the wrong cable plugged in because it was in a tangle.
3. **Open the USB/Connectivity tab** — Once it opens, click the USB/Connectivity icon (second from the bottom).
4. **Find the controller** — Find the controller on the device list. You may just have to click on each option while moving the joysticks so you can see the feedback on screen.
5. **Set the driver controller** — When you find the controller, move it to position zero, at the top. This will make it the driver's controller (the one that moves the drivetrain around).
6. **Set the operator controller** — If you have a second controller, then move it to position one, second from the top. That will make it the operator's controller. This one, generally, moves things like the collector, arm, shooter, turret or whatever that year's game brings.
